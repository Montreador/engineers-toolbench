Constant flux — full breakdown torque available

Three things to try: (1) sweep frequency from 2 to 50 Hz and watch the curve slide left, keeping its height — constant flux, constant torque. (2) Push past 50 Hz into field weakening: voltage is pinned, so flux and breakdown torque fall away as 1/f² — you buy speed by spending torque. (3) Set frequency low (say 5 Hz) with boost at 0 and watch the curve sag below the ideal ghost — the stator's own resistance eats the tiny output voltage. Wind boost up and the curve climbs back onto the ideal. Too much boost, though, over-fluxes it — the status goes red.
