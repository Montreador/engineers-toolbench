What to watch: add load and see how the two pointers separate. On the induction rotor the copper pointer keeps drifting backwards — it never catches the field, and the wider the gap, the more rotor heat (watch the bar climb). Switch to PM or reluctance and the rotor locks to the field: load just tips it back by a fixed load angle δ, speed stays exactly synchronous, and rotor loss sits at zero. Push load past 1.0 and the synchronous rotor pulls out — δ passes 90°, it loses the field entirely and falls away. That's the synchronous machine's version of breakdown.
