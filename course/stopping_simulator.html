Pick a method and press Run.

Race them: set inertia high, choose Ramp, no resistor and a short ramp — the bus rockets straight into the trip line and the drive faults before the load is anywhere near stopped. Same settings on Ramp + brake resistor: the resistor fires at 750 V, pins the bus there, and the stop completes fast — the kinetic energy is now heat in the resistor. Coast is gentle on the bus but slow, and dumps everything into friction. DC injection keeps the bus calm too, but the energy goes into the rotor as heat, and you'll see the braking fade as it nears standstill — it's a stopper, never a holder.
