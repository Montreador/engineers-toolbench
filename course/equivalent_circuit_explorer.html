Two demonstrations to run: First drag R₂ and watch the breakdown dot slide left along the speed axis while its height never changes — s_m carries R₂, T_max does not. That single fact is the whole principle of wound-rotor / slip-ring starting: bury resistance in the rotor to drag the peak (and the starting torque) toward standstill. Then drop voltage and watch the entire curve collapse as V² against the grey nominal ghost — a 10% sag costs ~19% of torque, which is why an undervoltage quietly walks a loaded motor toward stall.
