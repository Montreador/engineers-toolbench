What to watch: in the steep stable region near synchronous speed, huge changes in torque cost only a sliver of speed — that's why a healthy motor holds ~1450 rpm whatever the load. Push the load past the breakdown peak and there's no stable point left: the operating dot falls off the cliff to zero speed. That's a stall — and notice the current pins to its locked-rotor value (~6.5×). Every drive fault family lives on this one curve: overcurrent is the left-hand wall, overload is creeping too far down the stable slope, and the breakdown peak is the ceiling the drive must never ask the motor to cross.
