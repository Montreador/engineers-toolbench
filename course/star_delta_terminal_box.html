The terminal block — link bars decide it

The bottom row is labelled W2 · U2 · V2 — deliberately shifted. That stagger is what lets the same three bars make star when laid flat and delta when stood upright. Turn the bars 90°, change the motor.

The trick in one line: every coil is happiest at 230 V — and 230 V delta and 400 V star both deliver exactly that. Same motor, two supplies, identical winding voltage. Now try the one that bites people: 400 V + delta. The coils get the full 400 V — √3 over rated — and the verdict goes red. That single mis-link, delta on a 400 V supply when the plate says 230/400, is a genuine motor-killer: it looks wired, it even runs for a moment, then it cooks.
