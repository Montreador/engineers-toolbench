The whole idea in one screen. Up top, in the frame that spins with the rotor flux, the current is just two steady numbers: I_d holds the flux, I_q makes the torque, and they sit at 90° like a DC motor's field and armature. Slide I_q and torque tracks it instantly while flux doesn't budge — that decoupling is the entire point of FOC. Down below is the same current as the motor really sees it: three AC waves whose size and phase come straight from that d/q split. The Park/Clarke transform is the bridge between the calm top panel and the spinning bottom one — and it's the reason a modern winder can hold full tension at a dead stop.
